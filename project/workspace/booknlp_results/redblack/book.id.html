Julien is only a carpenter's son, but dreams of following in the footsteps of his (Julien) hero, Napoleon. However, in Julien's time, men gain power in the Church and not in the army. Valenod, the mayor's political adversary, finds out about the affair and begins to spread rumors. His (Julien) aristocratic benefactor, the Marquis de la Mole, wants M. Pirard to be his (Mole) personal secretary in Paris, but M. Pirard tells him (Mole) to hire Julien instead. The Marquis then refuses to let Mathilde marry Julien, who furiously returns to Verrières and shoots Mme. de Rênal. She (Mathilde) survives, but Julien is sentenced to death anyway.
