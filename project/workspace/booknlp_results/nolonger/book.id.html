Obi Okonkwo is a young man, about twenty-six years old, who returns to Nigeria after studying in England at a university for four years. No Longer At Ease, begins with a trial against Obi that takes place a while after his (Obi) return, and the novel then works its way backward to explain how Obi has come to be charged with accepting a bribe. The Umuofia Progressive Union -LRB-U.P.U-RRB- has given Obi a scholarship to study law in England, a scholarship that Obi has to pay back upon his (Obi) return. And, thus, he (Obi) leaves for England, stopping in Lagos on the way out. While in England, several things happen to him (Obi). Finally, he (Obi) meets a girl named Clara at a dance in London but fails to make a good impression. However, the girl is Nigerian also, and on Obi's boat ride back home, after nearly four years in England, he (Obi) meets Clara once again. This time, they begin a relationship. Once back in Nigeria, Obi stays, once again, in Lagos with his (Obi) friend Joseph, trying to find a job and a place of his (Obi) own. He (Obi) also visits his (Obi) own home village of Umuofia. Obi is quickly given a post on the Scholarship Board of the Civil Service and is also quickly introduced to the world of bribery, which is a world he (Obi) wholeheartedly rejects with a strong idealism at first.
