Tod Hackett has been recruited from Yale School of Fine Arts to work as a set and costume designer for National Films in Hollywood. When the novel opens, Tod has been in Hollywood for only three months and still marvels at the people and architecture of the city, both of which involve blatant and constant artifice and masquerading. Tod is most interested in the section of the population that does not seem to be masquerading--the imported, lower middle-class Midwestern immigrants who stand around the city and stare at the masqueraders. Abe helped Tod find his (Tod) current apartment, which Tod only decided to take upon seeing Faye Greener, who lives downstairs. Tod desires Faye, but she (Faye) has unsentimentally told him (Tod) that they must remain polite friends, as Tod has no money and is not particularly good-looking. Tod hopes that his (Tod) chances with Faye have improved now that Faye's father Harry has fallen ill and Tod visits with the man nightly.
