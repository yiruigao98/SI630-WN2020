Text

Two men, Vladimir and Estragon, meet near a tree. They converse on various topics and reveal that they are waiting there for a man named Godot. While they wait, two other men enter. Pozzo is on his (man) way to the market to sell his (man) slave, Lucky. He (man) pauses for a while to converse with Vladimir and Estragon. Lucky entertains them by dancing and thinking, and Pozzo and Lucky leave. After Pozzo and Lucky leave, a boy enters and tells Vladimir that he (Vladimir) is a messenger from Godot. He (Vladimir) tells Vladimir that Godot will not be coming tonight, but that he (Vladimir) will surely come tomorrow. Vladimir asks him (Vladimir) some questions about Godot and the boy departs. After his (Vladimir) departure, Vladimir and Estragon decide to leave, but they do not move as the curtain falls. The next night, Vladimir and Estragon again meet near the tree to wait for Godot. Lucky and Pozzo enter again, but this time Pozzo is blind and Lucky is dumb. Pozzo does not remember meeting the two men the night before. They leave and Vladimir and Estragon continue to wait. Shortly after, the boy enters and once again tells Vladimir that Godot will not be coming. He (Vladimir) insists that he (Vladimir) did not speak to Vladimir yesterday. After he (Vladimir) leaves, Estragon and Vladimir decide to leave, but again they do not move as the curtain falls, ending the play.
