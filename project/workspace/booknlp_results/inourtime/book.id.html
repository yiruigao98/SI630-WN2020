Text

In Our Time is a collection of short stories and vignettes about the years before, during, and after World War I. The stories, which are titled, are separated by vignettes, each of which is a chapter. The first story,

``On the Quai at Smyrna,'' (unknown)
introduces the war through a description of an evacuation. Then,
``Indian Camp,'' (Nick/Nick Adams)
``The Doctor and the Doctor's Wife,'' (Nick/Nick Adams)
``The End of Something,'' (Nick/Nick Adams)
``The Three-Day Blow,'' (Nick/Nick Adams)
``The Battler'' (Nick/Nick Adams)
and perhaps
``A Very Short Story'' (Nick/Nick Adams)
all describe an encounter in the life of Nick Adams. Through these stories, Nick seems to be growing up. The vignettes are scenes from war, in which few characters are named. Chapter VI, after
``The Battler,'' (Nick/Nick Adams)
is about Nick in the war. After that, the vignettes turn away from stories of the war and the short stories take up a different subject.
``A Soldier's Home'' (a soldier)
is about a soldier named Krebs returning to his (Krebs) hometown.
``Mr. And Mrs. Elliot,'' (couples)
``Cat in the Rain,'' (couples)
and
``Out of Season'' (couples)
are all about American couples living or vacationing in Europe.
``Cross-Country Snow'' (men)
is about two men -LRB-one of whom is named Nick-RRB- skiing together.
``My Old Man'' (a jockey son)
is about a jockey and his (jockey) son. Then,
``Big Two-Hearted River,'' (Nick/Nick Adams)
parts I and II, are about the return of Nick Adams to his (Adams) fishing ground, the forest around which and town of which have been burnt down. The vignettes that separate these stories vary in content but mostly deal with bullfighting. The final vignette,
``L'Envoi,'' (a king)
is a surreal story about a visit to a Greek king.
