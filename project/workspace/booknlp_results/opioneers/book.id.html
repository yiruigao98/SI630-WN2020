O Pioneers! opens on a blustery winter day, in the town of Hanover, Nebraska, sometime between 1883 and 1890. The narrator introduces four principal characters: the very young Emil Bergson; his (Emil) stalwart older sister, Alexandra; her (Alexandra) gloomy friend Carl Linstrum; and a pretty little Bohemian child, Marie Shabata. From town, Emil and Alexandra and their neighbor Carl return home to the desolate stretch of plains known as the Divide. Alexandra's father, John Bergson, is dying. He (Bergson) tells his (Bergson) two eldest sons, Oscar and Lou, that he (Bergson) is entrusting the farmland, and the preservation of all that he (Bergson) has accomplished since his (Bergson) immigration from Sweden, to his (Bergson) daughter. It becomes clear that Alexandra is stronger and more resolute than her (Alexandra) brothers. When drought and depression strike three years later, Alexandra's determination allows her (Alexandra) to persevere. Many families, including Carl Linstrum's, sell their farms and move away. But Alexandra believes in the promise of the untamed country, and so she (Alexandra) convinces her (Alexandra) brothers to re-mortgage their farm and buy more land. She (Alexandra) also convinces them to adopt innovative farming techniques. The narrative jumps sixteen years into the future, when Alexandra's faith in the land has been repaid. Lou and Oscar are married, and each owns his (Lou) own farm. Alexandra's farm is the most prosperous on the Divide. Emil has been provided the wealth and luxury to leave the Divide for the State University. Crazy Ivar, the elderly, slightly imbalanced man who, earlier in the novel, gave Alexandra some controversial farming advice, now works in Alexandra's stables, although Lou and Oscar disapprove of his (Lou) presence. Meanwhile, Marie Shabata is trapped in an unhappy marriage with a sullen and difficult husband, and it becomes clear that she (Shabata) and Emil are falling in love. Before he (Emil) can leave, though, tragedy strikes: his (Emil) best friend, Amedee, dies unexpectedly. At a church mass, Emil enters a state of rapture and resolves to say farewell to Marie. He (Emil) finds her (Marie) in a similar ecstasy in her (Marie) orchard, and he (Emil) lies down next to her (Marie). Her (Marie) jealous husband, Frank Shabata, finds them.
